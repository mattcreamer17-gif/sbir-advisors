You have moved 70+ clients through SBIR and STTR capture since late 2020. Over $600M in awards. A partner catalog for government buyers, AWS Marketplace placements, and a Phase III transition layer. 02

The Eduba frame

Our working view on any consultancy your size: about 60% of what your team does is traditional software and database work that somebody is currently doing in Word, email, and Google Sheets. Another 30% is rule-based logic (compliance checks, topic matching, budget math, data-rights clauses). Only 10% is genuine AI, and that 10% is where generative help on first-draft proposal narrative, BAA summarization, and client-tech-to-topic fit actually pays off. Teams that skip this distinction end up with an LLM wrapper where Postgres and a decision tree would have done the job faster, cheaper, and with better guarantees. An orchestration layer respects those boundaries.

03

The case

We built a multi-agent system for a compliance-heavy operator working in the DRC. The team was burning 160+ hours per month producing regulator-ready documents from messy inputs. After the orchestration layer shipped, that same work took under 5 minutes, with a human approving the output rather than generating it. The architecture is the same shape you need: inspectable context, layered memory, and a review gate at every step a contracting officer could later audit.
